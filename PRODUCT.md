# Product

## Register

brand

> A wedding site mixes emotional surfaces (Home, story, places) with functional tools
> (chat, RSVP, gallery, admin). The default lens is **brand** — for guests, the feel of
> the site *is* part of the gift — but functional surfaces (Chat, RSVP, Admin, Quiz,
> Tables) are handled with a **product** lens per task: clarity and task completion lead,
> the premium skin serves the workflow.

## Users

Wedding guests of **Sofia & Marco** (wedding: 14 June 2027, Naples — ceremony at the Duomo,
reception at Villa Doria d'Angri). Mixed ages and tech comfort, mostly Italian with some
English speakers (the app is bilingual IT/EN). They open it on a phone, often in social
moments — to RSVP, browse the program and venues, chat, share and view photos/videos,
check the menu, play the quiz, find their table, and read FAQs. A small admin audience
(the couple) manages guests, RSVPs and invitations.

## Product Purpose

A single, beautiful home for everything about the wedding — countdown, program, venues,
RSVP, shared media, real-time chat, menu, gifts, games and an AI assistant — so guests
feel invited into the celebration rather than handed a form. Success = guests RSVP without
friction, return to the app repeatedly before and during the event, and come away feeling
the couple made something special for them.

## Brand Personality

**Luxury Wedding Social Network.** Romantic, elegant, modern, exclusive — the polish of a
well-funded consumer startup (Airbnb · Apple Invites · Notion Mobile · Instagram ·
Pinterest), not a wedding template. Voice is warm and intimate but confident and
uncluttered. Emotional goals: emotion, elegance, social warmth, a sense of being on a
short list of people who matter.

## Anti-references

- **2015-era wedding templates** — script fonts, faux-watercolour florals, "Wordpress
  matrimonio" look.
- **Kitsch** — hearts everywhere, overly bright/saturated colours, cheesy ornamentation.
- **Cold/corporate SaaS** — sterile enterprise cards, blue gradients, dashboard sterility
  on the guest-facing surfaces.
- **Loud / overdesigned** — gradients everywhere, decorative glassmorphism, visual noise.

## Design Principles

1. **The feel is the gift.** Every guest-facing screen should evoke emotion before it
   conveys information. Craft and restraint over decoration.
2. **Social, not form-like.** Lean into chat, photos, reactions, presence — make it feel
   alive and shared, like a private social network for the celebration.
3. **Premium through restraint.** One confident pink, generous whitespace, soft depth,
   real typography. Resist the urge to add; subtract until it feels expensive.
4. **Mobile is the product.** Designed phone-first; every interaction must feel native and
   effortful-free with the thumb. Desktop is a graceful widening, not the priority.
5. **Motion with intent.** 200–350ms, soft easing, no aggressive or bouncy animation;
   movement guides attention and rewards interaction, never distracts.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Bilingual (IT/EN). Target WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1 (use the deep-rose token, never the
light pink, for text on light backgrounds; muted grey meets AA on white). Honour
`prefers-reduced-motion` with crossfade/instant fallbacks for every animation. Touch
targets ≥44px. Mixed-age audience: avoid tiny type, keep labels explicit, don't rely on
colour alone for status (RSVP states carry icon + text).
